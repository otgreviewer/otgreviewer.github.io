Week 1 - The Atom, Stoichiometry I, Periodic Trends

Physical or Chemical Change
How to determine if an occurrence is a physical or chemical change? Just know the characteristics of chemical changes.
Characteristics of chemical changes:
  • Bubbles form, gas is released
  • Changes color
  • Releases an odor
  • Inability to reverse the change
  • Precipitation of a solid from a liquid solution.
Therefore, cooking/boiling of rice is an example of a chemical change

Periodic Trends
4 Main Periodic Trends
  • Atomic Radii - elemental radius.
  • First Ionization Energy - the minimum amount of energy required to remove an outermost electron to form an ion with a +1 charge.
  • Electron Affinity - add electron to the outermost with a -1 charge.
  • Electronegativity - the tendency of an atom to attract an electron to itself when its covalently bonded to another atom.

Week 3 - Solutions

Solubility - The ability of solute to dissolve in water.

Temperature ↑ Solubility ↑
Types of Solutions

Saturated - maximum solute that a solvent can dissolve
Unsaturated - less solute than saturated
Supersaturated - more solute than saturated; unstable
2 Different Concentrations (2 formulas)
Molarity - the number of moles of the solute dissolved in a liter of solution.
$$M=\frac{moles~of~solute}{liters~of~solution}$$

Molality - is a measure of the concentration of a solute in a solution in terms of amount of substance in a specified amount of mass of the solvent.
$$m=\frac{moles~of~solute}{kg~of~solvent}$$
Mole Fraction
$$X_{solute}=\frac{moles~of~solute(n)}{moles~of~solute(n)+moles~of~solvent(N)}$$
$$X_{solvent}=\frac{moles~of~solvent(N)}{moles~of~solvent(N)+moles~of~solute(n)}$$
Note: The sum of all the mole fractions is always equal to 1.

C. Freezing Point Depression

D. Osmotic Pressure
